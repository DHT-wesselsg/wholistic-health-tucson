Informed Consent for Treatment

Nature of Services

I understand that I will receive treatment within the scope of practice of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) and complementary healing modalities, which may include:

  • Acupuncture: The insertion of sterile, single-use, disposable needles through the skin at specific points on the body
  • Neurofeedback Therapy: EEG brain training using Brainpaint technology
  • Chinese Herbal Medicine: Dietary supplements and herbal formulas
  • Cupping: The application of glass or plastic cups creating suction on the skin
  • Gua Sha: Scraping technique using smooth-edged tools
  • Moxibustion: The burning of herbs near the skin
  • Tui Na: Chinese therapeutic massage
  • Lifestyle and Nutritional Counseling: Recommendations for diet, exercise, and lifestyle modifications
  • Electro-acupuncture: Mild electrical stimulation of acupuncture needles

Potential Benefits

Acupuncture and Traditional Chinese Medicine treatments may help with:

  • Pain relief and management
  • Stress reduction and emotional balance
  • Improved circulation and energy flow
  • Enhanced immune system function
  • Better sleep quality
  • Digestive health improvement
  • Hormonal balance
  • Overall wellness and preventive care

Potential Risks and Side Effects

While acupuncture and TCM treatments are generally very safe, I understand that there may be some risks involved, including but not limited to:

  • Minor bleeding or bruising at needle insertion sites (common)
  • Temporary soreness or aching at needle sites (common)
  • Temporary lightheadedness or dizziness (occasional)
  • Temporary fatigue following treatment (occasional)
  • Emotional release during or after treatment (occasional)
  • Burns or scarring from moxibustion (rare when properly administered)
  • Infection at needle sites (extremely rare with sterile technique)
  • Organ puncture (extremely rare with proper technique)
  • Marks or bruising from cupping or gua sha (expected and temporary)
