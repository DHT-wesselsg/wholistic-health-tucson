Serving Those Who Served

I have a deep reverence for those who have served and currently serve our beautiful country. Both of my grandfathers were veterans, and I've carried a patriotic spirit since childhood. This work is my way of giving back.

The opioid crisis hit our veteran community hard, and that is not something I take lightly. We know that opioids foster addiction and impede the body's natural ability to manage issues like insomnia while disrupting the gut biome. Acupuncture and cupping offer a different path ‚Äî they promote rest and relaxation by interrupting the pain signal to the brain, moving us away from stress, trauma, and pain without pharmaceuticals.

These modalities also improve the function of the gut, liver, kidneys, lungs, and other major organs. With each session, we're increasing resilience and decreasing vulnerabilities.

Here in Tucson, I have an incredible opportunity to offer natural solutions for veterans dealing with pain, PTSD, and other service-connected conditions. If you've never tried acupuncture, I welcome you to come in ‚Äî even if you're skeptical. Symptoms typically improve across sessions, but results can sometimes be immediate. I recently worked with a 91-year-old patient in extreme pain whose pain levels dropped to zero after the first session. It doesn't always happen like that, but it can and does.

An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure. If you're hearing an alarm bell going off in your body, please don't wait until you're experiencing a full-on health crisis. The time is now.

I am here to help you untangle confusing and sometimes scary health issues and get you back on track. I believe everyone deserves to live a balanced, peaceful, and pain-free life.

I'm also a proud supporter of Soldier's Best Friend, a nonprofit that pairs veterans with rescued dogs for service and therapeutic companionship. I keep their brochures in my office ‚Äî ask me about it at your next visit.

VA patients: I currently see veterans who schedule in person or by phone.
